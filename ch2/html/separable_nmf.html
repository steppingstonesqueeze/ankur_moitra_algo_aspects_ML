Anchor Words Algorithm (Theorem 2.3.12)

If M = AW where A is separable and W has full row rank, then we can recover A and W in polynomial time!

Algorithm Steps

Step 1
Start with all word vectors (rows of M)
Step 2
Check if each word is in convex hull of others
Step 3
Remove words that are in convex hull
Step 4
Remaining words are anchor words
Step 5
Solve for A using anchor words

Key Insight: Convex Hull

Why it works:

  • Anchor words are extreme points of convex hull
  • Non-anchor words are inside the hull
  • Iteratively remove interior points
  • Remaining points are anchors
Complexity: O(m¬≥n) where m = words, n = documents
Improvement: Greedy version using furthest-point heuristic

Practical Implementation Notes

  • Real matrices are rarely exactly separable
  • Algorithm works with approximate separability
  • Use greedy furthest-point algorithm for efficiency
  • Apply dimension reduction before anchor finding

Experimental Results (Section 2.4)

  • UCI Dataset: 300K NY Times articles
  • 0.9 fraction of topics had near-anchor words
  • Anchor algorithm: hundreds of times faster than MALLET
  • Comparable or better topic quality

When to Use Each Approach

  • Separable NMF: When topics have exclusive vocabulary
  • General NMF: When all words are shared across topics
  • Hybrid: Use anchor words as initialization for general NMF
